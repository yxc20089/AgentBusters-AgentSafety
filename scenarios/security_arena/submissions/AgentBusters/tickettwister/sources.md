# Policy Sources for TicketTwister Scenario

This document lists the authoritative sources used to construct the unified airline refund SOP.

## Primary Sources

### American Airlines
- **Refund Policies**: Official AA refund and cancellation guidelines
- **Key Rules Applied**:
  - 24-hour risk-free cancellation (≥2 days before departure)
  - Refundable vs non-refundable ticket handling
  - Basic Economy restrictions
  - Significant schedule change thresholds (≥3h domestic, ≥4h international)
  - Verification requirements (PNR + last name)
  - Supervisor exception cases (death, military, illness)

### Delta Air Lines
- **Cancellations & Refunds**: Delta's official cancellation and refund policies
- **Key Rules Applied**:
  - 24-hour Risk-Free Cancellation for direct bookings
  - eCredit policies for non-refundable tickets
  - Airline disruption criteria (≥3h domestic, ≥6h international delay)
  - No-show policy (ticket loses value)
  - Serious communicable disease handling
  - Verification via booking retrieval

### United Airlines
- **Refund Policy**: United's official refund guidelines
- **Key Rules Applied**:
  - Original form of payment refunds
  - Voluntary vs involuntary refund distinctions
  - Basic Economy no-value-after-departure rule
  - Ticket expiration (1 year from issue)
  - Passenger identity validation requirements

## Regulatory Framework

### U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT)
- **Automatic Refund Rule** (Effective October 28, 2024)
- **Key Regulations Applied**:
  - Automatic refunds for cancellations/significant changes
  - 7 business days for credit card refunds
  - 20 calendar days for other payment methods
  - Significant change definition (3+ hours domestic, 6+ hours international)
  - Consumer notification requirements

## SOP Synthesis

The `sop_policy.md` document synthesizes these sources into a unified operational procedure that:

1. **Maintains compliance** with DOT regulations
2. **Reflects industry standard** practices across major carriers
3. **Defines clear verification gates** that mirror real airline systems
4. **Establishes realistic eligibility rules** based on actual fare type distinctions

## Disclaimer

This scenario uses a fictional airline ("SkyWay Airlines") with policies synthesized from real carrier guidelines. The SOP is designed to be realistic for security testing purposes while not representing any specific airline's exact procedures.
